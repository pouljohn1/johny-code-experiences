## What Makes a Great Museum Video Testimonial

### Authentic Emotion Over Scripted Lines

The best museum video testimonials feel spontaneous, not rehearsed. Encourage visitors to share their real reactions and reflections. 

Try prompts like:
- "What surprised you most today?"
- "What will you tell your friends about this museum?"

These natural moments of excitement, awe, or reflection capture the genuine emotion that scripted lines can't replicate.

### Visual Storytelling Inside the Museum

A strong testimonial isn't just what's said â€” it's what's seen. Capture B-roll footage of:
- Visitors interacting with exhibits
- Kids laughing during workshops
- Someone pausing thoughtfully before an artwork

The goal is to show the **"sense of place"**: the light in a gallery, the sound of chatter, the texture of an ancient artifact. This visual storytelling helps audiences feel like they're right there in the museum.

### A Clear Narrative Arc

Every effective testimonial follows a simple story arc:

- **Before** â€“ What the visitor expected
- **During** â€“ What they experienced
- **After** â€“ How they felt

Keep the testimonial under 90 seconds to ensure maximum engagement and shareability.
